Understanding Policies

For foreign students in Japan, it is essential to ensure they have the correct visa and residency status to live legally in the country. Adhering to immigration laws and promptly notifying local authorities of any address changes is crucial. Additionally, students typically enter into rental agreements with landlords or housing agencies, which outline details such as rent, payment deadlines, lease duration, and the responsibilities of both parties. It is vital for students to thoroughly read and comprehend these agreements, seeking clarification when needed.

Landlords may require a financial guarantee from a guarantor, often a Japanese national. Students without guarantors can explore options like utilizing a guarantor service or paying a non-refundable fee. When signing a lease, students might be asked to provide a security deposit and key money. Key money is generally non-refundable and serves as a payment to the landlord, while the security deposit is refundable upon the termination of the tenancy.

Regarding maintenance and repairs, landlords typically handle significant issues, while tenants should promptly report any problems or damages. Tenants may be responsible for repair costs resulting from negligence or intentional damage. It is crucial for students to review lease termination conditions and notice requirements. Typically, one or two months' notice is expected, and tenants should return the unit in a neat and undamaged condition, excluding usual wear and tear.

In Japan, strict no-smoking policies are often enforced in apartments, including the building premises. Prospective tenants who smoke should carefully review the smoking policy of the apartment before renting. Respecting neighbors' privacy and complying with noise ordinances, especially during quiet hours, is strongly encouraged. Disturbances and loud noises may violate the terms of the rental agreement.

Kyoto, Japan places significant emphasis on waste management, and tenants must adhere to strict guidelines for garbage disposal. Waste should be divided into categories such as organic, recyclable, burnable, and non-burnable. It is important to follow the designated collection schedule for each type of waste. Specific instructions for sorting various waste materials should be observed. Burnable rubbish should be placed in appropriate orange or transparent bags, while non-burnable waste should be appropriately bagged or placed in containers. Recyclable materials should be rinsed and sorted into designated containers. For the disposal of large objects, tenants should arrange separate collections by contacting the local municipality. Illegally dumping waste may result in penalties.
